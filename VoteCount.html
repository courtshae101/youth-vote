The Youth Vote: By the Numbers

ALooking at the states with top 20 largest youth populations, we there is a pretty even distribution of Demoncratic and Republican states. Out of these eligible youth voters, we see a sharp decrease in people who actually vote in the 2016 election. In 2018, a Midterm election year we see even fewer young people cast their ballots. It is normal for not all eligible voters to cast their vote, for example in 2016 only 61.4% of eligible voters voted and in 2018 only 53% voted. However youth voting numbers are much lower than the national average, 38% of eligible young voted and in 2018 only 28.2% voted.
